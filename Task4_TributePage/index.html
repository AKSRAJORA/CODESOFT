Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India, The People's President

About

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was widely regarded as the "People's President" for his affable nature and his connection with the youth. Before his presidency, he worked as an aerospace engineer with Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts. He is known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and nuclear weapons programmes.

Early Life and Education

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a Tamil Muslim family. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a homemaker. To support his family, young Kalam sold newspapers. He graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, in 1954 and pursued a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), Chennai, graduating in 1960.

Scientific Career

After graduating from MIT, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the DRDO as a scientist. He later moved to ISRO, where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). He played a pivotal role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite 'Rohini' which was successfully launched in July 1980. Kalam also spearheaded the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles and was instrumental in the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

Presidency (2002–2007)

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam served as the 11th President of India, winning the 2002 presidential election with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party and the then-opposition Indian National Congress. During his term, he was affectionately known as the "People's President." He focused on connecting with the youth and emphasized the importance of education, science, and technology for national development. His interactions with students across the country were a hallmark of his presidency.

Awards and Honors

  • Padma Bhushan (1981)
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • Bharat Ratna (1997) - India's highest civilian award
  • Several honorary doctorates from universities across the globe.

Later Life and Legacy

After his presidential term, Kalam returned to education, teaching and lecturing at various institutions. He continued to advocate for science, technology, and innovation, and his focus remained on empowering the youth of India. He authored several influential books, including *Wings of Fire*, *Ignited Minds*, *India 2020*, and *Turning Points*. His life and work continue to inspire millions, and he remains a revered figure in India and beyond. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
